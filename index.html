Working Paper · February 2026

"Graph-Based Stackelberg Model for Adversarial Supply Chain Enforcement"

A. Bose · February 2026

Formalizes semiconductor diversion as a Stackelberg security game on a directed supply-chain graph. An enforcement agency commits to a probabilistic inspection policy while an adversary adapts routing subject to capacity and budget constraints. The model yields tractable equilibria that inform risk-prioritized inspection allocation.

Game Theory Supply Chain Export Controls Stackelberg
Working Paper · Under Review at ICML 2026

"Impact of Advanced AI Agents on the Enforcement-Circumvention Balance in Export Controls"

A. Bose · February 2026

Analyzes how agentic AI capabilities expected to mature by 2027–2029 differentially affect enforcement and circumvention actors in semiconductor export controls. Proposes a capability taxonomy and scenario-based assessment framework for reasoning about how automation asymmetrically affects each side of the enforcement equation.
